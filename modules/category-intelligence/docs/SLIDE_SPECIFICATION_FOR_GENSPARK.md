# Google Slides Presentation Specification for Genspark AI
## 3M Garage Organization Category Intelligence

**Document Purpose:** Complete slide-by-slide specification for Genspark AI to create Google Slides presentation
**Total Slides:** 35 slides (25 main + 10 appendix)
**Presentation Duration:** 45-60 minutes
**Audience:** 3M Cross-Functional Innovation Team

---

## Presentation Design Guidelines

### Theme & Visual Identity
- **Primary Color:** Charcoal (#2C3E50)
- **Accent Color:** Teal (#16A085)
- **Background:** White with subtle texture
- **Typography:**
  - Headings: Montserrat Bold
  - Body: Open Sans Regular
  - Accent: Montserrat SemiBold

### Layout Principles
- **Generous white space** - avoid cluttered slides
- **Visual hierarchy** - large headings, clear structure
- **Consistent iconography** - use simple line icons
- **Data visualization** - clean charts/diagrams, minimal decoration
- **Photography** - authentic, not stock; consumer reality

### Content Density Rules
- **Maximum 3 bullet points** per slide (with sub-bullets if needed)
- **Maximum 1 major visual** per slide
- **One key message** per slide
- **Footnotes** in small text at bottom when needed

### SLIDE 33: Market Weighting Methodology
**Layout:** Title + calculation visualization

**Title:** "Market Weighting Calculation: Correcting for Walmart Over-Sampling"

**Content:**

**The Problem:**
- Dataset: Walmart = 78.5% of products
- Market Reality: Walmart = ~15% of revenue
- Over-sampling creates bias if not corrected

**The Solution: Market Weighting**

**Formula visualization:**
```
Weight Factor = (Market Share ÷ Dataset Share)

Walmart: 0.15 ÷ 0.785 = 0.20x weight
Home Depot: 0.35 ÷ 0.098 = 3.57x weight
Lowe's: 0.30 ÷ 0.037 = 8.11x weight
Amazon: 0.15 ÷ 0.052 = 2.88x weight
Target: 0.05 ÷ 0.026 = 1.92x weight
```

**Example Application:**
Average price calculation uses weighted values, not raw dataset percentages

**Design Notes:**
- Formula should be visually clear
- Before/after comparison showing correction impact
- This builds trust in data rigor

**Source Material:** 04_APPENDIX_DATA_CITATIONS_AND_AUDIT_TRAIL.md, Calculation Methodologies

### SLIDE 34: BSR-to-Sales Estimation
**Layout:** Title + methodology explanation

**Title:** "Amazon BSR-to-Sales Estimation Methodology"

**Content:**

**What is BSR?**
Best Seller Rank - Amazon's ranking of product sales velocity in a category

**Conversion Formula:**
```
Monthly Sales = 10,000 × (BSR ^ -0.85)

Where:
- 10,000 = estimated monthly sales for #1 ranked product
- -0.85 = decay factor (empirically derived)
- BSR = Product's rank in "Tools & Home Improvement > Garage Organization"
```

**Example:**
- Product ranked BSR 100 → ~290 units/month
- Product ranked BSR 1,000 → ~40 units/month

**Confidence Level: MEDIUM**
- Conversion rates vary by season and category
- Used for directional estimates, not precise forecasts
- Applied to top 20 products to estimate ~$500K monthly revenue

**Design Notes:**
- Formula breakdown with annotations
- Example calculations visible
- Confidence level prominent
