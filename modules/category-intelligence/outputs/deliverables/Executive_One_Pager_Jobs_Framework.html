Consumer Jobs Framework: Strategic Blueprint for Lighting Innovation
How to Think About Consumer Needs, Prioritize R&D, and Build a Unified Innovation Pipeline

The Challenge We Face

We optimize for features (lumens, adhesive strength, dimming) without understanding the actual jobs consumers are trying to complete. This creates misalignment: Marketing promotes ease of installation, R&D focuses on technical specs, and Retail sees products returned because consumers couldn't finish the project. We lack a shared language to unite around consumer needs and prioritize investments that solve complete jobs—not just individual pain points.

The Jobs-to-be-Done Framework

This analysis identifies 5 core consumer jobs based on 79 video interviews with actual consumers installing lighting. These aren't features they want—they're complete outcomes they're trying to achieve. Each job has measurable emotional intensity, market prevalence, and strategic tier. This framework provides the unifying structure to align Marketing messaging, R&D roadmaps, Retail training, and Analytics KPIs around what actually drives consumer behavior, retention, and category expansion.

How This Framework Unifies Our Work

For R&D: Build complete job solutions, not feature improvements. Electrical complexity requires more than better instructions—it needs visual guides, retail training, or hardware simplification. For Marketing: Message around jobs consumers recognize ("Never call an electrician mid-project") not specs they don't understand. For Retail: Train associates to diagnose which job a consumer is solving. For Analytics: Track job completion rates, not just purchase conversion. The full report provides detailed consumer insights, sub-job breakdowns, and verbatim quotes with video evidence for each job. This is the foundation for strategic alignment.
