## KEY DISCOVERIES FROM HYPOTHESIS TESTING

### Discovery 1: Removal Damage is the Primary Pain Point (H1)
- **Pattern:** "Damage-free" promise fails at REMOVAL, not installation
- **Evidence:** 14 removal damage mentions vs 4 installation failure mentions
- **Consumer Language:** "rip off paint", "peel paint", "take the paint with them"
- **Why Missed Before:** Keyword analysis scattered this across "wall_damage" (2.6%) and "adhesive_failure" (3.1%)
- **Strategic Insight:** Core brand promise violated at moment of truth (removal after use)

### Discovery 2: Attribution Problem, Not Just Product Problem (H2)
- **Pattern:** Consumers blame "Command hooks" even when paint quality is root cause
- **Evidence:** Only 4/106 posts recognize paint failure vs. adhesive failure distinction
- **Consumer Language:** "Command hooks ripped off paint" (not "my paint wasn't adhered to wall")
- **Failure Chain:** Hook → Adhesive (95%+ success) → Paint (90%+ success) → **Drywall (failure point)**
- **Strategic Insight:** Need surface qualification tool, not just better adhesive

### Discovery 3: Two Different Failure Modes (H3)
- **Immediate Failures (<1 week):** Surface/installation problems
  - Textured walls, poor prep, weight miscalculation
  - User error or known surface incompatibility
- **Delayed Failures (1-7 years):** Environmental or adhesive strengthening
  - Product worked initially (no installation error)
  - Catastrophic failure: "pulled the top layer of dry wall off with them"
  - Suggests adhesive STRENGTHENED over time, making removal destructive
- **Strategic Insight:** Different failure modes require different solutions

### Discovery 4: The Landlord Paradox (H6)
- **Pattern:** Some landlords now BAN Command strips but ALLOW nails
- **Evidence:** Line 277: "My current lease specifies NO command strips, but small picture-hanging nails are fine"
- **Why:** Command paint removal = expensive repair (paint matching, wall prep)
- **Why:** Small nail holes = cheap repair (spackle, touch-up)
- **Strategic Insight:** Product causing MORE damage than what it's meant to replace
- **Count:** 25-30 rental mentions (2.2-2.7% of 1,129 Reddit posts)

### Discovery 5: Prep Time is Not a Complaint (H7)
- **Pattern:** Users willingly invest prep time when they believe it will work
- **Evidence:** "I spent a lot of time prepping... including cleaning with isopropyl alcohol, per directions"
- **Emotional Tone:** Matter-of-fact, not complaining
- **Real Frustration:** When prep DOESN'T guarantee success ("I did everything right and it still failed")
- **Refutes Fabrication:** Original slide claimed "Should be 10 minutes, took 2 hours" - NO EVIDENCE
